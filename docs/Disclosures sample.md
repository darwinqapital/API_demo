Event Contracts Information and Risk Disclosure Statement

By funding your account and placing orders for Event Contracts, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and accepted the risks outlined below.

1. Nature of Event Contracts
Event Contracts (also known as prediction market contracts, forecast contracts, or binary options) are standardized derivative contracts whose settlement value is based strictly on the occurrence or non-occurrence of a specific, defined event at or before a specified time.

Contracts are typically structured as a "Yes" or "No" proposition.

If your chosen outcome occurs according to the contract's resolution criteria, the contract settles at a predetermined payout (e.g., $1.00 per contract).

If your chosen outcome does not occur, the contract expires worthless ($0.00).

2. High Risk of Total Loss
Trading Event Contracts involves a high degree of risk and is not suitable for all investors. The outcome of an Event Contract cannot be known in advance. You should be prepared to lose the entire amount of your investment. Unlike traditional equity investments that may retain partial value during a downturn, an incorrect prediction in an Event Contract results in a 100% loss of the capital staked on that specific trade.

3. Pricing and Market Risk
The price of an Event Contract (e.g., trading between $0.01 and $0.99) reflects the market’s aggregate belief regarding the probability of the event occurring.

Pricing is not guaranteed: Market prices may not reflect the actual real-world probability of an event occurring.

Volatility: Prices can fluctuate rapidly based on news, rumors, or shifts in market sentiment. Changes in the actual likelihood of an event may not immediately or accurately translate to a change in the contract’s trading price.

4. Liquidity and Execution Risk
Event Contracts may have varying levels of liquidity. [Insert Platform Name] does not guarantee a continuous secondary market for any given contract.

You may be unable to buy or sell a contract at your desired price or volume.

Limited liquidity may prevent you from exiting or offsetting a position before the contract expires, forcing you to hold the contract through to resolution and exposing you to total loss.

5. Source Agency and Resolution Risk
The resolution of an Event Contract depends entirely on the outcome reported by a specific, predefined third-party data source or authority (the "Source Agency"), as detailed in the contract's specific rulebook.

Inaccuracies: If the Source Agency reports inaccurate, delayed, or incomplete data, your contract will settle based on that data, regardless of real-world realities.

Disputes: [Insert Platform Name] settles contracts strictly according to the underlying contract rules. We are not liable for losses resulting from data security breaches, errors, or methodological changes made by the Source Agency.

6. Fully Collateralized (No Margin)
Unlike traditional futures or options, Event Contracts on [Insert Platform Name] are fully collateralized. You cannot trade on margin. You must have the full maximum potential loss amount available in your account balance to open a position. Once the position is fully margined, no further capital calls will be made for that specific position.

A trading halt will prevent you from entering or exiting positions, which may adversely affect your trading strategy.

You are exposed to operational risks, including hardware/software failures, network outages, and cyber-attacks that may delay or prevent the execution of your orders.

8. Regulatory and Legal Risk
